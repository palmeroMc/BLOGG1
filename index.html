Bit-by-Bit Programming

by Mary Christelle Palmero

Choosing Computer Science major as someone with little to no background felt like I was one of the strongest soldiers ni Lord. “Malaki sweldo” raw kasi kaya palag na (ewan nalang ngayon). If you're someone like me, then happy reading! Join me on my struggles.

First laboratory activity was printing “Hello World.” I was a bit tense because I couldn’t recall any syntax, but thankfully my seatmate knew some. After that day, I started scanning information seriously. Slowly, nababawasan yung kaba ko. Not confident yet, but progress is progress.

When activities became harder, the thought kicked in: “Para sa’kin ba talaga ‘tong CS?” Almost every day. But here I am — 2nd semester, 2nd year. Logic everywhere. Minsan tulong from Brother GPT and his relatives. Dati ang goal ko lang makapasa. Ngayon gusto ko na talagang maintindihan.

CS is wide. Everything is interconnected. You can’t skip fundamentals. That’s when I decided to restart — basics. Loops. Concepts. Slowly connecting everything. It’s overwhelming if you don’t track progress. So kahit mabagal, I chose to move forward bit-by-bit.

Sikmura pa ba 'to?

by Mary Christelle Palmero

Never in my life na nasabihan akong maarte sa pagkain. My fam will only call me out whenever I eat weird food combinations. "Ano bang klaseng sikmura meron ka?" Kahit ilang food combos na yung nagagawa ko, madalas gulat parin sila. In the end, nginingitian ko na lang sila.

When I was a kid, I tend to pair my rice meals with hot drinks lalo na kapag breakfast. Hindi lang 'to drink' but to pour it on rice making it sinabawan meal. Seems bearable naman para sa lahat kaso it came to a point na hindi lang sa rice nahahalo yung mga drinks. I remember soaking Cream-O cookies sa orange juice, I was delightful. My sister called me, and asked me why would I do that. Yes it's kinda gross to imagine, but in my defense, soaking cookies in juices is same thing as eating them separately, mine's only faster. IFYKYK.

My favorite foods to eat are chicken and tuna dishes. Usually, the chicken is paired with ketchup/gravy/mayo/toyomansi if it's fried or roasted. However, kapag ang ulam namin ay tinola, my taste buds seem to ask for more flavor. Don’t get me wrong, I’m not a picky eater nor my mom’s cooking is terrible, I just have weird food pairings. The missing flavor that my tongue needs for my Tinola is something I can get from soy sauce… Not fish sauce or chicken cubes–soy sauce. I like to eat the chicken bits dipped in soy sauce before feasting on its broth and Sayote. I may have call it Tinola Pro Max. Next food combo I’d like to mention is my go to tuna rice meal. Spicy San Marino corned tuna with a sweet twist. I rarely eat spicy corned tuna on its own, due to my low spicy tolerance, kapag naubusan ng red, no choice na but to go for the spicy one. It’s not that bad pero I knew it can be a better meal. That’s when I discovered adding enough amount of milk (Bear Brand for best combo) is the one that’ll make the meal to the next level. Not too much of it but just enough to cover the rice and tuna. After trying that, dumalas na yung cravings ko sa meal na yun. I have plenty of combo meals pero those two are my topmost favorites.

To some, it is challenging for their tummy situation to try or experiment new food combos that’s why most of the time it’s easy for them to judge a food combo by its look only. I have experienced some trouble na rin naman pero rarely. Nevertheless, I, myself is the one I need approval for, no other than. As long as it satisfies me and it doesn’t affect my stomach, I couldn’t care less to what other people think of my food combos. My sikmura, my choice.

Are You Winning Self?

by Mary Christelle Palmero

Taking the tech track in college used to be my “safety net” career in the future because I grew up having a business mind to pretty much everything I do. Now, I felt the will to embrace the tech and settle with it. Nakakatuwa lang din dahil for the first time in my life naging desido rin ako sa gusto kong tahakin sa buhay.

Before taking up computer science, I had little knowledge about the potential jobs/careers after finishing college—Developer, Web Designer, Data Analyst, and Technicians. Even with little knowledge, I decided to pursue computer science with a plan of being a Data Analyst afterwards. I wasn’t 100% decided on being a Data Analyst, all I knew is that I need to choose something tech related. As I’ve mentioned, I grew up having business as my main career goal. I believe it’s influenced by my situation of being a tindera sa Sari-Sari store ni mama ever since I was a kid. I have always found myself negotiating with other people whenever they transact with me with any product or service. Maski paninda ni mama binebenta ko rin sa school ko dati para may dagdag baon. But, everything changed when I learned deeper information regarding the possible career track in the tech field. I had this long talk with my relative regarding how people who pursued tech in college or any related tech, most of them succeeded in life by being a system engineer. I got intrigued with how they worked and soon grabbed my interest in being one. To this day, I aspire to be a system engineer, although I still have no specific industry I want to partake in yet, I believe that it’s something I need to work on because being a system engineer can’t be replaced by AI– or at least in the near future.

I may have decided on an ideal plan in my head right now, I still won’t rely on a single plan solely. All that matters now is that I am making progress and I am willing to devote my time, mind, and heart in achieving something. Regardless of what my career will be, all I hope is for it to be helpful for my growth as an individual. To myself 5 or 10 years from now, I hope you’re winning in life.
